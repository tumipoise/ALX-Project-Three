Part 1 - Exploratory Analysis of Loan Data from Prosper¶

by Tumininu Adesimbo Sosanya¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I studied loan features that could aid in determining the borrower's rate and loan status. In terms of loans, my primary focus was on the prosper risk score, the borrower's monthly income, and the borrower's employment status.¶

Dataset Overview¶

The 113,937 loans in the data set used for this study have an average of 81 variables, including loan amount, borrower rate (or interest rate), current loan status, borrower income, and many others. However, only 5 features were used because they were relevant to my analysis, and about 30,000 missing entries were removed because the data was collected before July 2009 and did not include prosper score data ( a very important variable for my analysis) The prosper score is a custom risk score built using historical Prosper data that is used to scale loans based on how risky or less risky they are. The risk score ranges from 1 to 10, with 10 being the best. Loans originated are eligible.¶

Loan status, Prosper Risk Score , Employment Status¶

With regard to defaulted, completed, and charged-off loans, we can state with certainty that the risk score for unemployed people's loans always lags behind that of all employed people. This indicates that the most risky current, completed, and charged-off loans are collected by unemployed people, while the most risky past-due loans were collected by employed people, as the risk score for employed people shows.¶
